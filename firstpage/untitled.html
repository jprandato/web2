Linguine with Crab, Lemon, Chile, and Mint

Serves 6-8

Cooking Time 30min

Prep Time 30min

Ingredients

  1. 8 ounces linguine
  2. Kosher salt
  3. 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  4. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  5. ¼ cup minced shallots
  6. 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  7. 2 Fresno chiles seeded, sliced into thin rounds
  8. 1 ½ tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  9. Freshly ground black pepper
  10. 8 ounces cooked, shelled Dungeness crab, king crab, or jumbo lump crabmeat
  11. ⅓ cup fresh mint leaves, gently torn

Materials

  1. Large Boiling Pot
  2. Medium Skillet

Instructions

  • Cook pasta in a large pot of boiling salted water, stirring occasionally, until al dente. Drain, reserving 1 cup pasta cooking liquid.
  • Meanwhile, melt 1 Tbsp. butter with 1 Tbsp. olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add shallots and stir until just soft. Add garlic and 1 chile and cook, stirring often, until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add ½ Tbsp. lemon juice and 3 Tbsp. pasta cooking liquid to shallot mixture; add a pinch of pepper. Stir until liquid is almost evaporated.
  • Transfer pasta to skillet and add ½ cup reserved pasta cooking liquid. Increase heat to medium high. Cook until liquid is almost evaporated, about 2 minutes.
  • Add the remaining 3 Tbsp. butter, 1 Tbsp. oil, 1 Tbsp. lemon juice, 1 tsp. lemon zest, crab, half of mint, and more chile rounds. Stir pasta until butter melts and pasta is well coated. Divide between bowls; top with remaining 1 tsp. lemon zest and mint. Sprinkle with more lemon juice if desired.
